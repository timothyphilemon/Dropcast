TV Installation Guide

Get JukeCast running on your Fire TV in about 5 minutes. No computer required.

Step-by-step setup
1
Enable Developer Options on your Fire TV
Go to Settings → My Fire TV → About and click the Fire TV name 7 times rapidly until you see "You are now a developer."

Then go to My Fire TV → Developer Options and turn on Apps from Unknown Sources.
2
Install the Downloader app
Go to the Amazon App Store on your Fire TV and search for "Downloader" — it's a free app by AFTVnews. Install it.

When you first open it, it will ask permission to install unknown apps. Tap Settings, enable the toggle, then go back to Downloader.
3
Download and install JukeCast
Open Downloader and type this URL in the address bar:
jukecast.tv/jukecast.apk
Tap Go. The APK will download automatically. Once complete, tap Install, then Done.
4
Open JukeCast and note your session ID
Find JukeCast in your apps list and open it. Your 6-digit session ID will appear on screen for 15 seconds — note it down. This is the ID you'll use to connect this TV to your dashboard.
5
Add the TV in your dashboard
Go to your venue dashboard, click Add TV, and enter the 6-digit session ID from the TV. This links the TV to your account permanently.
6
You're live
Your dashboard will show a ● Connected status for that TV. Customers can now go to jukecast.tv, enter your session ID, and start casting.
✓
JukeCast runs quietly in the background. When a customer queues a video it wakes up, plays it, then returns to whatever app was running — Pluto TV, Tubi, Atmosphere TV, or whatever you set in your dashboard.
Troubleshooting
TV shows "Not connected" in dashboard
Make sure JukeCast has been opened at least once and the session ID was entered. Also confirm the TV has an active internet connection.
Downloader says "not allowed to install unknown apps"
Tap the Settings button in the prompt and enable the toggle for Downloader. Then go back and try again.
Wrong session ID entered on TV
Uninstall and reinstall the JukeCast app — the setup screen will appear again on first launch so you can enter the correct ID.
Videos won't play
Make sure the TV has a strong internet connection. Some YouTube videos block third-party playback — try a different video to confirm.
Can't find Downloader in the App Store
Search exactly "Downloader by AFTVnews" — it's a free, well-known app used by millions of Fire TV owners for sideloading.
